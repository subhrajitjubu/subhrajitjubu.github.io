About Me

I am an atmospheric researcher specializing in AI-driven climate and weather modeling with a focus on integrating deep learning into radiative and cloud parameterizations. I develop end-to-end machine learning pipelines—primarily convolutional neural networks (CNNs)—for rainfall and cloud-fraction forecasting using multi-dimensional inputs: thermodynamic variables, dynamical fields (e.g., geopotential height, divergence), and cloud microphysical parameters. Model architectures are ResNet-inspired and tuned via SPC-based hyperparameter searches, and planning to be deployed within operational NWP systems through a bespoke Fortran–PyTorch interface for tight coupling with global models. My computational expertise includes designing high-performance Fortran solvers for radiative transfer leveraging HITRAN datasets, and implementing numerical solutions to advection–diffusion equations using finite-difference, . I use Python (PyTorch, NumPy, Xarray, SciPy) and MATLAB for predictive modeling, numerical experiments, and large-scale data preprocessing, and run HPC workflows on Linux.

My research sits at the intersection of atmospheric physics, machine learning, and scientific computing, with active interests in AI for cloud microphysics, deep-learning emulation of radiative transfer, hybrid physics–ML parameterizations, large-scale Earth-system modeling, ML interpretability in climate models. My technical toolbox includes Fortran, Python, MATLAB, and radiative-transfer models such as MODTRAN and RRTM, deployed on Linux and Windows.
