About Me

As a researcher, I leverage expertise in curating HITRAN molecular data for seamless integration into radiation models, ensuring meticulous cleaning, organisation, and formatting. Proficient in Fortran, I have developed advanced computational methods and algorithms to solve infrared radiative transfer equations, enabling high-fidelity simulations of atmospheric radiative processes. Additionally, my research has unveiled crucial relationships between meteorological factors (temperature, humidity, wind shear) and cloud microphysics, enhancing predictive capabilities. I also possess expertise in implementing the advective diffusion equation via numerical techniques (finite difference, finite element, spectral methods), facilitating accurate modeling of substance transport in fluids, with a technical toolbox encompassing Fortran, Python, MATLAB, and specialised radiation transfer models (e.g., MODTRAN, ARTS) on Linux, Windows.
